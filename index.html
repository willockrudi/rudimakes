How It Works

The Repair Process

From first contact to finished repair — here's what to expect.

1

Reach Out

Email me with what you've got and what's wrong. Include the make, model, and symptoms. I'll let you know if I can help and give you a rough idea of timeline.

2

Drop Off

Bring your gear to my shop in Indianapolis (by appointment) or to Jonas Productions. I'll do an intake and confirm the reported issue.

3

Diagnosis

I put it on the bench, trace the fault, and figure out what's actually going on. Then I contact you with findings, options, and a cost estimate before any repair work begins.

4

Repair & Test

Once approved, I do the repair, test thoroughly, and document everything. Standard turnaround is 3–7 days. Rush service available.

5

Pickup

I let you know when it's ready. You get your gear back with a written summary of the work and a 30-day labor warranty.

FAQ

Common Questions

How much does a repair cost?

All repairs start with a $45 bench/diagnostic fee, which gets applied to your total if you proceed. Labor is billed at published hourly rates (typically $55–65/hr depending on the work), plus parts at cost + 25%. I always confirm the estimate before starting. See full pricing.

How long does a repair take?

Most repairs are completed in 3–7 business days. Complex jobs or parts-dependent repairs may take longer — I'll give you a timeline estimate at diagnosis. Rush service (48-hour turnaround) is available for an additional $35.

What brands do you work on?

All of them. Fender, Marshall, Vox, Mesa/Boogie, Orange, Peavey, Ampeg — and boutique or vintage amps of any make. For synths: Moog, Roland, Korg, Sequential, Yamaha, Oberheim, and more. If it has circuits, bring it in.

Do you work on solid-state amps?

Yes. While tube amps are my specialty, I troubleshoot and repair solid-state amplifiers too. Same diagnostic approach, same documentation.

Can I ship my gear to you?

I primarily do local drop-off in Indianapolis. For special cases (rare gear, out-of-state clients), reach out by email and we can discuss shipping arrangements.

What if my gear isn't worth repairing?

I'll tell you. If the cost of repair exceeds the value of the gear, I'll let you know upfront so you can make an informed decision. You only pay the bench fee for the diagnosis.
